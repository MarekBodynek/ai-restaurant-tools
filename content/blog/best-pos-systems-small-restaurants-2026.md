# Best POS Systems for Small Restaurants in 2026

Choosing the right point-of-sale system can make or break a small restaurant. A good POS handles orders, payments, inventory, and staff management — all while keeping things simple enough that your team actually uses it.

We reviewed dozens of restaurant POS systems and narrowed it down to the **7 best options for small restaurants** in 2026, based on pricing, ease of use, features, and real-world reliability.

## 1. Toast — Best Overall for Small Restaurants

**Pricing:** Free Starter plan available; Growth plan at $69/month

Toast was built specifically for restaurants, and it shows. The free Starter plan is genuinely usable — not a stripped-down demo. You get online ordering, basic reporting, and a hardware terminal at no upfront cost (though you'll pay higher payment processing fees on the free tier: 2.99% + $0.15 per transaction vs. 2.49% + $0.15 on paid plans).

**Key features:**
- Purpose-built restaurant hardware (spill-resistant, heat-tolerant)
- Built-in online ordering and delivery management
- Kitchen display system (KDS) integration
- Payroll and team management add-ons
- Extensive third-party integrations (DoorDash, Uber Eats, etc.)

**Pros:**
- Restaurant-specific from the ground up
- Free plan is actually usable for small operations
- Excellent kitchen workflow tools

**Cons:**
- Locked into Toast's payment processing
- Hardware is proprietary (can't use your own tablet)
- Long-term contracts on some plans

**Best for:** Small to mid-size restaurants that want an all-in-one system purpose-built for food service.

## 2. Square for Restaurants — Best for Tight Budgets

**Pricing:** Free plan available; Plus at $60/month

Square's restaurant POS is the easiest way to start taking orders with minimal upfront investment. The free plan includes basic floor plan management, table management, and menu management. You can even use your own iPad — no proprietary hardware required.

**Key features:**
- Free online ordering page
- Built-in delivery integration
- Real-time sales and labor reporting
- Open API for custom integrations
- Works on iPad, Square Terminal, or Square Register

**Pros:**
- Genuinely free tier with no monthly commitment
- No long-term contracts
- Easy to set up (most restaurants are live in under an hour)
- Flexible hardware options

**Cons:**
- Fewer restaurant-specific features than Toast
- Advanced features (like course management) require Plus plan
- Processing fees are non-negotiable (2.6% + $0.10 in-person)

**Best for:** New restaurants, food trucks, cafés, and anyone who wants to start with zero upfront cost.

## 3. Clover — Best for Quick-Service Restaurants

**Pricing:** $14.95–$84.95/month depending on plan

Clover offers sleek, modern hardware and a straightforward interface that works well in fast-paced environments. The Clover Mini and Clover Flex are compact enough for small counters, and the system handles high-volume quick-service operations smoothly.

**Key features:**
- App marketplace with 300+ integrations
- Built-in customer loyalty program
- Employee shift management
- Offline payment processing
- Multiple hardware form factors

**Pros:**
- Attractive, durable hardware
- Large app marketplace for customization
- Good offline mode

**Cons:**
- Pricing can be confusing (varies by reseller)
- Some essential features require paid apps
- Hardware is proprietary to Clover

**Best for:** Quick-service restaurants, fast-casual spots, and counter-service operations.

## 4. Lightspeed Restaurant — Best for Multi-Location Restaurants

**Pricing:** Starting at $69/month

Lightspeed shines when you need to manage multiple locations from a single dashboard. Menu changes, pricing updates, and promotions push across all locations simultaneously. The reporting tools are particularly strong — you can compare location performance side by side.

**Key features:**
- Centralized multi-location management
- Advanced inventory with ingredient-level tracking
- Floor plan customization
- Built-in CRM and loyalty
- Extensive reporting and analytics

**Pros:**
- Excellent multi-location tools
- Ingredient-level inventory tracking
- Strong reporting suite

**Cons:**
- Higher starting price than Toast or Square
- Some features locked behind higher tiers
- Steeper learning curve

**Best for:** Restaurant groups, multi-location operators, and restaurants with complex inventory needs.

## 5. TouchBistro — Best for Full-Service Dining

**Pricing:** Starting at $69/month

TouchBistro runs as a hybrid system — the core POS runs locally on iPad (so it works even if your internet drops), while cloud features handle reporting and back-office tasks. It's designed for full-service restaurants with features like tableside ordering, course-by-course firing, and detailed table management.

**Key features:**
- Tableside ordering with iPad
- Course management and meal pacing
- Detailed table and floor plan management
- Staff scheduling and performance tracking
- Offline-capable local network

**Pros:**
- Works offline (local server architecture)
- Excellent tableside ordering experience
- Purpose-built for full-service restaurants

**Cons:**
- Add-ons (reservations, online ordering) cost extra
- Hardware limited to iPad
- Customer support can be inconsistent

**Best for:** Full-service restaurants, fine dining, and establishments where tableside ordering matters.

SpotOn has quietly become one of the best value propositions in restaurant POS. Their entry-level plan includes features that competitors charge extra for — things like online ordering, loyalty programs, and review management. Processing rates are competitive at 1.99% + $0.25 for in-person transactions.

**Key features:**
- Built-in online ordering (no commission fees)
- Loyalty and review management included
- Labor management tools
- Marketing automation
- Competitive processing rates

**Pros:**
- More included features at lower price points
- No commission on online orders
- Competitive processing rates
- Good customer support

**Cons:**
- Less brand recognition than Toast or Square
- Hardware requires custom quote
- Fewer third-party integrations than larger competitors

**Best for:** Small restaurants that want maximum features without paying for multiple add-ons.

Revel is the enterprise-grade option that scales down reasonably well for ambitious small restaurants. If you're planning rapid growth or handle high transaction volumes, Revel's robust infrastructure won't buckle under pressure. The open API makes custom integrations straightforward.

**Key features:**

**Pros:**

**Cons:**

**Best for:** High-volume restaurants planning to scale, or operations needing deep customization.

## How to Choose the Right POS for Your Restaurant

### Consider your restaurant type
- **Quick-service / fast-casual:** Clover or Square
- **Full-service dining:** TouchBistro or Toast
- **Multiple locations:** Lightspeed
- **Food truck / pop-up:** Square
- **High-volume:** Revel or Toast

### Watch out for hidden costs
POS pricing is rarely as simple as the monthly fee. Factor in:
- **Payment processing fees** (typically 2.3%–3.0% + per-transaction fee)
- **Hardware costs** ($0–$2,000+ depending on setup)
- **Add-on features** (online ordering, loyalty, payroll)
- **Contract termination fees** (some charge thousands to cancel early)
- **Installation and training** (free with some providers, $500+ with others)

### Questions to ask before signing
1. What's included in the base plan vs. paid add-ons?
2. Can I use my own payment processor, or am I locked in?
3. What happens if my internet goes down — does the POS still work?
4. How long is the contract, and what's the cancellation fee?
5. What does onboarding/training look like?

## The Bottom Line

For most small restaurants in 2026, **Toast** offers the best combination of restaurant-specific features, reasonable pricing, and long-term scalability. If budget is your primary concern, **Square for Restaurants** lets you start for free with no commitment. And if you need a system that shines at tableside service, **TouchBistro** is purpose-built for that experience.

Whatever you choose, make sure you test the system during a trial period with your actual menu and workflow before committing to a contract.
